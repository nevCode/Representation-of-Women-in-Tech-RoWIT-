Ada Lovelace



Born in the 18th century in the year of 1815 and died at a young age of 36 Ada Lovelace is recognised as the worlds first programmer. She work as an inventor for the world renown Charles Babbage and through that employment is where her concept of computing operating system stemmed from.


Grace Hopper



A prolific pioneer in computer science she is known for creating the COBOL which is the first computing language as well as she helped to devise the UNIVAC I, the first commercially used electronic computer.


Sheryl Sandberg



The first female board member and current chief operating officer of Facebook. She has also founded Leanin.org a non profit organisation that is dedicated to helping women achieve their goals by offering ongoing inspiration and support.


Margaret Hamilton


Her accolades includes developing the guidance and navigation system that helped to put man on the moon by leading the NASA software team. She is also created with coining the discipline that is Software Engineering.


Sister Mary Kenneth Keller



The first female in the United States to receive a Phd degree in Computer Science in the year 1965. She died in 1971 but she left a legacy behind due to her founding a computer science department and establishing a masters degree program for applications in computer science.


ENIAC



ENIAC stands for Electronic Numerical Integrator and Computer. It is the first programmable general purpose electronic digital computer that was built during the Second World War. The ENIAC’s first programmers were that of six women who learned to program at a time that programming languages and tools didn’t exist. They successfully enabled the ENIAC to run ballistics trajectory in a matter of seconds.
